Abstract

"Water quality issues persist as a critical global concern, impacting communities, ecosystems, and economies worldwide. The 21st century has witnessed an escalation in the challenges associated with maintaining clean and safe water sources, as highlighted by Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 6, which emphasizes the crucial need for ensuring universal access to clean water and sanitation. In response to the global challenge of water quality degradation, this study aims to create a Water Quality Management Device utilizing date pits and Raspberry Pi Pico as main components. The date pits, with their antimicrobial potential and high removal efficiencies due to their high surface area and pore volume, make them effective for water filtration technology. Additionally, the Raspberry Pi Pico is a fast and flexible microcontroller board used for controlling other portions of an electronic system and is useful for controlling reading sensors and monitoring tasks. The results of the study proved the effectiveness of the Water Quality Management Device in terms of contaminant removal and accuracy in assessing the water quality in terms of pH level, turbidity, and total dissolved solids (TDS)."
